## Report Disclaimer

**Important — Read Before Proceeding**

This report is prepared by ClearDeed using publicly available government land records. It is not a legal opinion, not a title certificate, and not a guarantee of ownership or freedom from encumbrance.

**What this report does:**
- Summarizes information found in public government databases (Bhulekh, Bhunaksha, eCourts, IGR)
- Flags inconsistencies between what you were told about the plot and what the official records show
- Identifies court cases registered against the claimed owner in Khordha district
- Identifies proximity to regulated zones (forest, coastal, tribal, environmental)
- Directs you to specific questions to ask the seller, the broker, and a qualified lawyer

**What this report does NOT do:**
- Certify that the seller is the legal owner of the plot
- Confirm that the plot is free from mortgages, liens, or legal disputes
- Guarantee that the plot is free from fraud, misrepresentation, or pending litigation
- Recommend that you buy, sell, or refrain from buying the plot
- Substitute for the judgment of a qualified property lawyer

**Legal basis for this disclaimer:**
- ClearDeed is an information aggregator, not a certified title search provider
- Public land records in India are maintained by state governments under the Odisha Land Revenue Records Rules
- Ownership in India is established through a chain of title documents (sale deeds, mutations, inheritances, court decrees) extending back to the state record of rights or a grant from the state
- A single RoR (Record of Rights) entry does not, by itself, constitute marketable title
- Only a qualified property lawyer with access to original title documents, registered deeds, and court records can certify title

**Before you transact:**
1. Share this report with a qualified property lawyer before signing any agreement
2. Request original title documents from the seller going back at least 30 years
3. Visit the Sub-Registrar office to obtain an Encumbrance Certificate (EC) for the plot
4. Verify the plot's boundaries on the ground with a local survey
5. Confirm that the land classification permits your intended use (residential, commercial, agricultural)
6. Confirm that no conversion of land use is required

**Data sources and timestamps:**
Every fact in this report is attributed to its source portal, with the timestamp when the data was retrieved. Records may have changed since that timestamp. Request fresh records from the relevant portal before finalizing any transaction.
